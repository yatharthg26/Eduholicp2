GRE

The Graduate Record Examinations (GRE) serves as a pivotal standardized assessment that numerous graduate schools across the United States, Canada, and select international institutions consider as a part of their admissions criteria. Managed and overseen by the Educational Testing Service (ETS), the GRE caters to global candidates eyeing advanced studies such as master’s, business specializations, MBA, JD, or doctoral programs. The Graduate Record Examinations (GRE) serves as a pivotal standardized assessment that numerous graduate schools across the United States, Canada, and select international institutions consider as a part of their admissions criteria. Managed and overseen by the Educational Testing Service (ETS), the GRE caters to global candidates eyeing advanced studies such as master’s, business specializations, MBA, JD, or doctoral programs. A multitude of graduate, business, and law institutions recognize and accept scores from the GRE General Test. Designed for digital delivery, the GRE® General Test evaluates a candidate's prowess in verbal reasoning, quantitative reasoning, critical analysis, and written articulation.

Test Duration: Approximately 3 hours and 45 minutes.
